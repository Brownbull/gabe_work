# Xmartlabs: A software engineer's deep dive into the Uruguayan product studio

**Xmartlabs is a bootstrapped, 13-year-old digital product studio with an exceptional open-source reputation but compensation below US market rates.** For engineers prioritizing technical growth, work-life balance, and collaborative culture over maximizing salary, this Montevideo-based company offers compelling opportunities. Their libraries power apps like Waze, Amazon, and Dropbox, placing them among GitHub's top Apple ecosystem developers. However, Glassdoor's **3.3/5 compensation rating** signals a significant consideration for candidates accustomed to US-level pay.

---

## Company profile reveals a mature, profitable operation

Founded in 2012 by **Agustin Bonaglia (CEO), Enrique Galindo (COO), and Martin Barreto (CTO)**, Xmartlabs operates from headquarters in **Montevideo, Uruguay** with a secondary presence in San Francisco. The company has grown organically to approximately **100-200 employees** across Uruguay, Argentina, and Colombia without raising external funding—a notable indicator of sustainable profitability.

Their Crunchbase growth score of **91/100** (up 5 points recently) suggests continued momentum. As a "boutique Product Development Studio," they partner with startups backed by Y Combinator, Sequoia, and First Round Capital, as well as established enterprises like ID.me, Tastemade, and CipherHealth. The business model combines staff augmentation and end-to-end product development, with project budgets ranging from **$30,000 to $350,000+** according to Clutch reviews.

### Open source contributions set them apart

What truly distinguishes Xmartlabs is their **212 GitHub repositories** powering over **40,000 apps** including household names:

- **Eureka** (11,800+ stars): The de facto iOS form builder, used in 15,000+ apps
- **XLPagerTabStrip** (7,000+ stars): Android-style paging for iOS
- **Bender** (1,800+ stars): Custom neural network framework that outperforms Apple's CoreML in benchmarks
- **XLForm** (5,800+ stars): Legacy Objective-C predecessor to Eureka

Co-founder Martin Barreto is recognized in the "Top 10 Swift OSS contributors on GitHub." This commitment to open source isn't just PR—it reflects a genuine engineering culture where knowledge sharing is embedded in daily work.

## Engineering culture emphasizes quality and continuous learning

The development approach follows **Agile methodology with two-week sprints**, lean startup principles, and design sprints for rapid prototyping. Code quality receives serious attention: employees consistently highlight thorough code reviews, pair programming sessions, and well-documented standards in their feedback.

Every new hire receives a **senior mentor**, and the company provides weekly "investment time" dedicated to learning, experimentation, and knowledge sharing. Engineers are encouraged to contribute to the technical blog, speak at conferences, and maintain open-source projects. Recent talks include appearances at AppBuilders, BASwiftable, and Mobile Day conferences.

For MLOps specifically, the team follows rigorous practices including experiment tracking with ClearML, dataset versioning, and reproducibility requirements—indicating mature data science operations rather than ad-hoc implementations.

## Work environment offers flexibility with geographic constraints

### Remote-first with genuine flexibility

Xmartlabs operates a **fully distributed model** with employees across Uruguay, Argentina, and Colombia. The "workation" policy allows working from anywhere, and flexible scheduling is consistently praised in employee reviews. Teams align with **Eastern Time Zone** for US client convenience.

The Montevideo office (Bv. España 2529) offers a pet-friendly environment for those preferring in-person work, with hybrid arrangements available. Management emphasizes trust over micromanagement—time tracking via Toggl serves billing purposes rather than surveillance.

### Benefits paint a mixed picture

- ✅ **Strong positives**: Gym membership, free food Fridays, 13th-month salary (standard in Uruguay), after-office activities, professional development support
- ⚠️ **Noted weaknesses**: Some employees describe perks as underwhelming compared to expectations; health and PTO details aren't prominently advertised

### Geographic limitations matter

Most positions require **LATAM residency**, with some specifically requiring Uruguay location. International candidates outside these regions face limited options, though senior roles occasionally offer more flexibility.

### What employees love
Recurring praise centers on code quality standards, helpful colleagues, clear objectives, schedule flexibility, and exposure to cutting-edge technology. Multiple reviews describe Xmartlabs as feeling "like a second home" with genuine camaraderie.

### Candid concerns
Compensation emerges as the consistent pain point. Junior developers reportedly earn **$1,000-2,000 USD monthly**—competitive within Uruguay's local market but significantly below what remote-first US companies pay. One reviewer explicitly noted this gap, though acknowledged it's industry-wide in Uruguay rather than Xmartlabs-specific. Some employees also mentioned project variability (occasionally "boring" client work) and limited career advancement pathways.

## Conclusion: Strong technical culture with a compensation tradeoff

Xmartlabs presents a compelling option for engineers who prioritize **learning, work-life balance, and meaningful technical work** over maximizing immediate compensation. The open-source pedigree demonstrates genuine engineering excellence, and the 93% employee recommendation rate suggests authentic satisfaction despite salary concerns.

**Best suited for**: Engineers in LATAM seeking a reputable, stable employer with strong technical culture; those valuing flexibility, mentorship, and open-source contribution opportunities; mid-career professionals looking to work on varied client projects across industries.

**May not fit**: Candidates prioritizing US-market compensation; engineers outside LATAM seeking traditional employment; those wanting highly structured career ladders or predictable project assignments.

The 13-year track record, profitable bootstrap model, and continued growth suggest a stable platform for career development—provided expectations around compensation align with regional market realities rather than US remote-work standards.
